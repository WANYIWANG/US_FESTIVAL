Memory of Festival in United State

Many of us seek community and connection, and there are few things that bring humanity together more than shared experience. Festivals, with their culture appeal and sense of organized gathering, offer this in concentrated form. From cool music to cutting-edge art, every year America supporting a rich amount of festivals. However, it seems like festivals are getting cancelled for one reason or another. The current festival graveyard is filled with bankrupted companies, poor ticket sales, loss of venues, lack of permit, severe weather, and other factors that lead to the cancellation of events. Memory of festival is for showing the tremondance festivals of the United States and to further analyze those festivals that have disappeared or cancelled by various reasons.

Introduction

Festival can illustrate different dimensions and cultures, religious in origin, festivals became secular in the early modern period when astute political actors from kings to revolutionaries began to map them onto political activity. In modern society, festivals greatly promote the sense of social cohesion. Many of these celebrations focus on cultural or interest-based themes and seek to promote their traditions to community members. They let community elders share stories and experiences; set up templates to maintain unity between families. By digging into the topic of festival, I find out there are so many festivals have been cancelled or have slipped out of the popular eyes. How might we re-recognize and re-pay attention to the festival, and effectively retain the festival culture is an issue worth exploring. In this data visualization project, I simply present some of the insights found in the limited amount of data that I collected.

Data Collection

I starting by search about dataset, it turns out there is no ready-made and one-time crawlable data online, so I need to manually collect festival data from wikipedia. I looked at some of festivals website page, and careful select the information I need for the next step visualization. The whole data collection process is: Festival list in US from Wikipedia → Click into festival website link → Find basic information → Add variables into spreadsheet. Nailed it.

I collected 622 lists of data. The reconsidered dataset contains 11 variables: Festival name, festival type, state, city, hold month,founded time, statues, reasons of cancellation, summarize of reasons of cancellation, stop time, and festival website link. ‘Statues’ in here been divided into three groups: Active represent festival still alive in US, Inactive means the festival is no longer hold anymore, and the active* festival is unstable festival that disappear for awhile and reactive. For my second map about disappearing festivals, I also need disappearing festivals geolocation. This part of data I fetched by python using geopy.geocoders. I eventually clean the data I got into different categories: The first dataset is all festivals I got with 11 variables, the second one is only contains disappearing festival’s (Inactive festival) information with longitude and latitude, the third one is only with state name and how many festivals it has for my first festival distribution map, and the fourth dataset is focus on the cleaned cancel reason of disappearing festivals.

From the dataset that I collected, I find out some interest part I did not notice before. The difference between festival, fest, and fair. The dataset showing diverse name of festivals, for example: there are very clear festival name like Long Beach Blues Festival, also some festival named as ‘HONK!’ that people can not understand what it is directly. The majority festivals name above the term of ‘Fest’, ‘Festival’, or ‘Fair’. In order to avoid ambiguity, I looked for the difference：

I unified these three kinds of activities into the category of generalized festival in my visualization, because even though from now fair can not represent as traditional or culture activity now, it can turns into festival by time. 2. The cancellation of the festival was mainly due to economic issues. Fundraising can be a tough thing for new founders. The cancellation of the festival is mainly due to financial situations. When it comes to creating a memorable festival, it must maintain a stable attendance and a strong supplier or support organization. Many festival founders revealed unbalanced revenue and expenditure management after the festival was cancelled.

Paper & Digital Prototype

All the data I collected can lead me to the way to tell what kind of story of festival. I begin with paper prototype of the visualization.

My first visualize decision is to show a dashboard with interactive legacy besides the festival distribution map, and follow up showing each state’s festival information. After consult professionals and peers opinion, I changed it into the dashboard idea into a scrolling page of four parts: A general introduction part to show some cancelled festivals and its information; a United State festival distribution by state; a bubble map that display disappearing festivals and its info box; one state story about cancelled festival.

Due to limited data, I cannot find and confirm the unification reason behind the cancellation of the festival, so I replaced the last part with the cancelled festival gallery to show five cancelled festival and the uncovered stories.

User Testing

I testing my visualization to peers and professionals, and got some insights for the next stages map exploration. In the first map, some of my user told me they do not see the difference between the state with no festival and the state with few festival, and it’s better to show festival with categories like film, music and so on. For the second map, I did not add the legacy of circle so users do not know what the size of circles mean, and I need to show the information box while user hover the circles. For both of the map, I was suggested change the basemap into a more vivid one to show the sense of festival, and reconsider the color of map. Another common error in choropleths is the use of raw data values to represent magnitude rather than normalized values to produce a map of densities. I understand it’s necessary to normalize the data under map, however, because the festival data is not only within one year, it’s need some time to normalize data in a time range. I will figure it out in my next step.
